About

Ramesh Bahadur Bist, Ph.D.

Incoming Assistant Professor of Artificial Intelligence for Integrated Food and Animal Systems

Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering, College of Agriculture and Life Sciences

Email: baeairlab@gmail.com

I am an incoming Assistant Professor for Integrated Food and Animal System at the North Carolina State University's Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering. I received my Ph.D. in Poultry Science from the University of Georgia in 2024 under the supervision of Dr. Lilong Chai. During my Ph.D. research, I worked extensively with AI tools and computer vision to detect, classify, and segment animal health, behavior, and welfare conditions in poultry. Additionally, we used a robotic dog to detect mortality in hens and locate floor eggs.

After completing Ph.D., I joined the University of Arkansas (UARK) in 2024 as a postdoctoral fellow, where I worked on computer vision and robotics research with Dr. Dongyi Wang. My work included developing an auto-labeling model and a text-prompt-based behavior detection model. I also applied various sensor technologies, such as hyperspectral imaging and thermal sensors to detect foreign materials in meat, woody breast condition, and spaghetti meat defects. In robotics, I contributed to building an unmanned ground vehicle capable of autonomous navigation inside poultry processing plants using simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM).

At NC State, I will leads a strong research and educational program focused on machine learning, robotics, automation, sensing technologies, and data-driven solutions for livestock farming. My goals include developing methods to predict and control livestock diseases, monitor animal health, build affordable unmanned vehicles, create mobile apps for real-time health tracking, apply machine learning to precision feeding and breeding, and design rapid, non-destructive food quality tests.

I have published extensively in journals such as Computers and Electronics in Agriculture, Artificial Intelligence in Agriculture, Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence, Frontiers in Robotics and AI, Poultry Science, and Journal of Environmental Management. I also currently serves as a guest editor for two special issues: “Application of Intelligent Systems in Poultry Farming” for Applied Sciences (MDPI) and “Precision Farming Technologies for Monitoring Livestock and Poultry” for AgriEngineering (MDPI). I am also an active reviewer for journals including Nature Scientific Reports, Artificial Intelligence in Agriculture, Computers and Electronics in Agriculture, Journal of Environmental Management, Applied Computing and Informatics, Poultry Science, Sensors (MDPI), ASABE Applied Engineering in Agriculture, and more..
